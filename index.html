Interdisciplinary

Fine Art & Computer Science

I aspire to combine computer science and fine art in multimedia.

When I was seven years old, I started learning fine art and joined one of the most profound art talent schools in Taiwan. During this period, I was trained professionally; I built up strong foundations in several kinds of art skills, including watercolor, sketching, calligraphy, and painting, which also allowed me to develop a superior aesthetic sense. Even though the training was tough then, I still enjoyed indulging myself deeply in the fascinating fine art environment. Because of possessing these experiences, I have strong art skills and a superior vision of aesthetics. In senior high school, I left art talent school and took the normal high school classes,

changing the field from fine art to science. In college, with the ability in fine art and exam scores, I attend the interdisciplinary program at the NTHU (National Tsing Hua University) and choose computer science to study. In college, I continuously sharpen my fine art skills, being a team leader of school art team and attend a number of extracurricular activities. In the future, I plan to combine computer science and fine art in the field of multimedia.
