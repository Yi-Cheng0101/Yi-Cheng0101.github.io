Hello, my name is Yi-Cheng Hsiao

With a background in software engineering and Computer Graphics, I specialize in real-time rendering, neural rendering, graphics programming, computer animation simulation, and 3D math. I consistently integrate technology and art to enhance the entertainment experience.

Professional Experience

I previously worked at HTC VIVE as a Graphics Software Engineer. During this time, I researched and developed technologies for real-time Streaming and a VR rendering application. One of the projects I engaged in involved developing VBS(VIVE Business Streaming), a streaming software in C++ and DirectX, which enables wireless or VIVE cable streaming of PC VR content from the computer to the VR headset and reduces the heavy rendering workloads.

I was a Research Assistant in Intelligent Geometric and Visual Computing (IGVC) Lab at Texas A&M University. My focus was on neural rendering and 3D Gaussians Splatting. This project had a strong research orientation, and I utilized 3D Gaussians Splatting techniques and photorealistic representations to generate 3D human avatar.

I was also a Research Assistant in Soft Interaction Lab and developing interactive media art and XR applications. There, I tested Igloo, a 360 degrees projector to visualize an immersive environment project using Unity and Touch Designer. And use Meta Quest3 and Hololens2 to create XR prototypes.

Academic Background

I am pursuing a Master of Science degree in Visualization from Texas A&M University, specializing in Computer Graphics. My coursework includes ray tracing, global illumination, real-time rendering, and programming, which has deepened my understanding of the real-time rendering and computer graphics. Additionally, my research includes developing a 3D photorealistic Gaussian Splatting Avatar controlNet for single video generate 3D human avatar and modify the human’s motion via skeleton controlling.

I studied Computer Science at National Tsing Hua University, specializing in computer graphics and high performance computing. My courses included physically based simulation, graphics programming application and linear algebra. Moreover, I attend many Student Supercomputer competitions around the world, ASC, ISC and HPC-AI.
